Chet Mancini

Software Engineer | Technical Consultant

M.Eng Student at Cornell University

My name is Chet Mancini. I'm a New Yorker in the general sense as I grew up in Rochester and just moved to NYC to work at Intent Media and start my career in the "Silicon Alley."

Before moving here I had a stint in Chicago doing a liberal arts undergrad at Wheaton College and after working as a developer at a nearby financial company. In my transition I spent a year in Cornell's M.Eng program which helped refine my conceptual skills and narrow my interests towards cloud computing, big data, and the challenging engineering problems surrounding these fields. My capstone project was building a distributed, fault-tolerant, scalable Python application framework which I hope to continue to improve over the next year.

I'm a Christian (Reformed Protestant) and have spent a lot of time over the past fifteen years thinking critically about beliefs and participating in the search for truth. Hard questions are the most worthwhile questions, whether that is "Is P=NP?", "How does the CAP theorem limit my application?", or "Who is God, or does he even exist?" There's a thin line to walk of always having something firm to believe but also keeping an open mind, as there is always more to learn. I hope to comment more on these challenges in my writing.

In addition to limited blogging, I have done a fair bit of teaching and tutoring of essential computers, basic programming, and I have TA-ed for a Discrete Math course. My hope is to give back to the NYC tech community through additional talks on data engineering.

Technically, I think software should be a pleasure to use when it's interfacing with humans, but I also think finding a faster algorithm to solve a problem is a pretty fun way to spend an afternoon. I believe in immutable data structures and first class functions.

I'm actually pretty chill with regard to most things, but he can be a bit of a snob when it comes to espresso.

Finally, in answer to your most pressing question, I love music but am not related to Henry.
